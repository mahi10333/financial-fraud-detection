# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, especially among students and online users. In this type of fraud, attackers trick users into transferring money by pretending to be trusted individuals or organizations.

A typical scenario begins when a victim receives a call or message from a fraudster claiming to be from a bank, e-commerce platform, or payment service. The fraudster may request sensitive details such as OTP, UPI PIN, or send a fake payment request link. In some cases, the attacker sends a “collect request” and convinces the victim that accepting it will result in receiving money, whereas it actually deducts money from the victim’s account.

Students and inexperienced users are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of digital payment security practices. Once the victim shares confidential information or approves the transaction, the money is instantly transferred and becomes difficult to recover.

The consequences of such fraud include financial loss, emotional stress, and loss of trust in digital platforms. This highlights the importance of awareness and secure practices while using online payment systems.

This case study connects with the Financial Fraud Detection project, as machine learning models can help identify suspicious transaction patterns and prevent such fraudulent activities in real time.
