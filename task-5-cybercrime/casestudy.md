# 📌 Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details. This type of fraud has increased rapidly due to the growing use of digital payments.

A typical UPI fraud happens in several steps. First, the attacker contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or fake advertisement, often pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or buyer/seller. Next, they create urgency or trust by offering rewards, refunds, or help with a problem. Then, they send a payment request or a malicious link and convince the victim to enter their UPI PIN, claiming it is required to receive money. In reality, entering the PIN authorizes a payment, resulting in money being deducted from the victim’s account.

Students, elderly people, and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment systems are the most common targets. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online systems.

To prevent such fraud, users should never share their UPI PIN, avoid clicking on unknown links, and verify the identity of callers. Awareness and careful usage of digital platforms can significantly reduce the risk of such cybercrimes.
